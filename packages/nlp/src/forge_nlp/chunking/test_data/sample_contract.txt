SECTION C — DESCRIPTION/SPECIFICATIONS/STATEMENT OF WORK

C.1 BACKGROUND

The Air Force Life Cycle Management Center requires an integrated software
platform for contract lifecycle management. The current manual processes for
tracking contract milestones, compliance requirements, and financial data are
inefficient and error-prone. The contractor shall develop, deliver, and
maintain a modern web-based application that automates these processes.

C.2 SCOPE

The contractor shall provide the following capabilities:
(a) Contract data management and tracking
(b) Automated compliance milestone monitoring
(c) Financial tracking and reporting
(d) Document management and version control
(e) Role-based access control and audit logging
(f) Integration with existing Government systems including FPDS-NG and SAM.gov

C.3 TECHNICAL REQUIREMENTS

C.3.1 System Architecture

The system shall be developed using modern web technologies and shall be
deployable to Government cloud infrastructure (AWS GovCloud or Azure Government).
The system shall support a minimum of 500 concurrent users and shall maintain
99.9% uptime during normal business hours.

The contractor shall implement a microservices architecture with the following
components: API gateway, authentication service, contract management service,
compliance monitoring service, document storage service, and notification
service. Each service shall be independently deployable and scalable.

C.3.2 Data Requirements

All data shall be stored in encrypted form at rest using AES-256 encryption.
Data in transit shall be protected using TLS 1.2 or higher. The system shall
maintain a complete audit trail of all data modifications, including the user
identity, timestamp, and nature of the change.

The database schema shall support the full lifecycle of federal contracts
from solicitation through closeout, including all modifications, options,
deliverables, and compliance milestones. The system shall support import and
export of data in standard formats including JSON, CSV, and XML.

C.3.3 User Interface Requirements

The user interface shall be responsive and accessible, conforming to Section
508 of the Rehabilitation Act and WCAG 2.1 Level AA guidelines. The interface
shall support modern web browsers including Chrome, Firefox, Edge, and Safari.
The system shall provide a dashboard view with configurable widgets showing
contract status, upcoming milestones, financial summaries, and alerts.

C.3.4 Reporting Requirements

The system shall generate the following standard reports:
(1) Contract Status Report — summary of all active contracts
(2) Financial Summary Report — funding status by contract and CLIN
(3) Compliance Dashboard — upcoming and overdue milestones
(4) Modification History Report — all modifications by contract
(5) Option Exercise Report — option periods and exercise deadlines
(6) Deliverable Status Report — deliverable tracking and acceptance

C.4 DELIVERABLES

The contractor shall deliver the following items in accordance with the
Contract Data Requirements List (CDRL) in Section J:

C.4.1 System Design Document (SDD) — due 30 days after award
C.4.2 Software Development Plan (SDP) — due 30 days after award
C.4.3 Monthly Status Reports — due 10th of each month
C.4.4 Test Plan — due 60 days after award
C.4.5 User Documentation — due with final delivery
C.4.6 Source Code Repository Access — continuous throughout performance
C.4.7 Final System Delivery — due NLT 31 December 2025

C.5 PERFORMANCE STANDARDS

The contractor shall meet the following performance standards:
- System availability: 99.9% during business hours (0700-1900 EST)
- Page load time: < 3 seconds for standard pages
- API response time: < 500ms for 95th percentile
- Data backup: Daily incremental, weekly full
- Incident response: Critical issues within 2 hours, major within 8 hours

SECTION M — EVALUATION FACTORS FOR AWARD

M.1 EVALUATION CRITERIA

Proposals were evaluated based on the following factors, listed in descending
order of importance:

Factor 1: Technical Approach (Most Important)
- Understanding of requirements
- Proposed technical solution
- Development methodology and schedule
- Risk identification and mitigation

Factor 2: Past Performance (Important)
- Relevance of past contracts
- Quality of deliverables
- Schedule performance
- Customer satisfaction

Factor 3: Cost/Price (Less Important Than Technical and Past Performance)
- Price reasonableness
- Price realism
- Cost risk analysis

M.2 EVALUATION METHODOLOGY

Technical proposals were evaluated on an adjectival rating scale: Outstanding,
Good, Acceptable, Marginal, and Unacceptable. Past performance was evaluated
on a confidence rating scale: Substantial Confidence, Satisfactory Confidence,
Neutral Confidence, Limited Confidence, and No Confidence.

Cost/price was evaluated for reasonableness and realism. The Government reserved
the right to award without discussions if the initial proposals were determined
to be fair and reasonable.

M.3 BASIS OF AWARD

Award was made to the offeror whose proposal represented the best value to the
Government, all evaluation factors considered. Technical merit was significantly
more important than cost/price in the source selection decision.
